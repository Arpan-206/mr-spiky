Three mechanisms. Not a metaphor.

1. LIF neurons

Leaky Integrate-and-Fire. Membrane potential accumulates from inputs, leaks over time, spikes at threshold.

Same abstraction as a biological neuron. Not a metaphor.

2. STDP

Spike-Timing-Dependent Plasticity. "Neurons that fire together wire together" — weighted by when they fired.

The learning rule your brain uses. Trained the SNN unsupervised.

3. Temporal streaming

The SNN reads your function line by line, one line = one timestep. Neuron state carries across lines.

Just like a reviewer whose gut fires at line 47 because of what they read through line 46.

45-second slide. Advance through the three cards deliberately. The through-line is: LIF gives the neuron a way to CARRY STATE. STDP gives it a way to LEARN without labels. Temporal streaming gives us CONTEXT-AWARE per-line scoring. These three together are what earn the "biological plausibility" claim.

A neuron reads your code

Membrane potential rises with each line's structural intensity. Threshold crossed → spike. That's the network noticing something.

Play the clip; narrate it briefly. "Each line the SNN reads bumps the membrane. The membrane also *leaks* between lines — that's the 'L' in LIF. Enough structural intensity in one line, it crosses threshold and spikes. That spike is what the API reports as a per-line score."

The training rule

Fire in the right order → connection strengthens. Wrong order → weakens.
Repeat 40,000 times on senior-authored code. That's the whole training.

"No gradient descent. No labels. No 'good/bad' annotation. Just letting neurons wire themselves according to what firing patterns naturally occur when the network is fed CPython, Django, FastAPI code. When training's done, the weights ARE the shape of senior code."

Six axes of "gnarly"

The network reads code as 13 structural features. We collapse its reaction into six human-readable axes on the response:

complexity deep nesting, branchy control flow
tangled_state variables reach across long distances
hidden_calls delegates to opaque calls; chains deep
exception_surface try/except/raise pressure
naming unusual identifier density
malformed doesn't parse — hard override

Every flagged line comes with a breakdown. You know what the network objected to, not just that it objected.

Move fast here — 30 seconds. Point at malformed: "This is the interesting one. Senior code always parses, so the network has never seen a parse error. That axis force-flags any line ast.parse chokes on. Everything else is emergent from STDP."
